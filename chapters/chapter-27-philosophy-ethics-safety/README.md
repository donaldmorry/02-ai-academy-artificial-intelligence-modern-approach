# Chapter 27: Philosophy, Ethics, and Safety

> **Source:** *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.), Chapter 27
> **Part:** Part VII: Conclusions
> **Estimated time:** 10–12 hours
> **Prerequisites:** Chapter 01: Introduction to AI; Chapter 21: Deep Learning (recommended)

---

## Chapter Overview

Examine philosophical foundations and limits of AI: consciousness, Chinese Room, symbol grounding, and strong AI claims. Study ethical frameworks for fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, and military use. Introduce technical AI safety: specification gaming, robustness, and alignment research directions.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Summarize major philosophical arguments about machine minds
2. Explain symbol grounding and embodied cognition challenges
3. Apply ethical frameworks to AI case studies (hiring, criminal justice, healthcare)
4. Analyze bias sources in data, models, and deployment contexts
5. Describe transparency tools: interpretability, explainability, auditing
6. Identify AI safety problems: reward hacking, distributional shift, adversarial examples
7. Evaluate policy proposals and organizational governance for AI
