# Chapter 28: The Future of Artificial Intelligence

> **Source:** *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.), Chapter 28
> **Part:** Part VII: Conclusions
> **Estimated time:** 9–11 hours
> **Prerequisites:** Chapter 27: Philosophy, Ethics, and Safety; Chapters 19–26 (at least two chapters from Part V–VI)

---

## Chapter Overview

Synthesize AI as integration of components: perception, reasoning, learning, language, and action. Survey emerging architectures (neuro-symbolic, world models, agentic LLMs), open research frontiers, and scenarios for near- and long-term AI development.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Decompose intelligent systems into interacting components
2. Describe neuro-symbolic integration approaches and motivations
3. Analyze agentic systems combining LLMs with tools and planning
4. Identify open problems: common sense, causality, continual learning
5. Compare optimistic and cautious forecasts for AGI timelines
6. Relate course techniques to capstone agent design
7. Articulate personal research or career interests informed by the full course
