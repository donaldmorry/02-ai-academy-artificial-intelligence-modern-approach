# Chapter 18: Multiagent Decision Making

> **Source:** *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.), Chapter 18  
> **Part:** Part IV: Uncertain Knowledge & Reasoning  
> **Estimated time:** 11–13 hours  
> **Prerequisites:** [Chapter 05 — Adversarial Search and Games](../chapter-05-adversarial-search-games/README.md); [Chapter 16 — Making Simple Decisions](../chapter-16-making-simple-decisions/README.md)

---

## Chapter Overview

Extend decision theory to multiple interacting agents. Study normal-form and extensive-form games, Nash equilibria, cooperative game theory, mechanism design, auctions, and voting theory. Connect competitive and cooperative multiagent AI systems.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Represent strategic interactions with payoff matrices and game trees
2. Find Nash equilibria in normal-form games
3. Analyze dominant strategies and iterated elimination
4. Explain cooperative games, Shapley value, and coalition formation
5. Describe mechanism design goals: incentive compatibility, efficiency
6. Apply auction types and voting systems to AI platform design
7. Relate multiagent decisions to adversarial search and RL self-play
