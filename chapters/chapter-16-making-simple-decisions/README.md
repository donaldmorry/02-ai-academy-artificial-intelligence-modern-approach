# Chapter 16: Making Simple Decisions

> **Source:** *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.), Chapter 16  
> **Part:** Part IV: Uncertain Knowledge & Reasoning  
> **Estimated time:** 11–13 hours  
> **Prerequisites:** [Chapter 13 — Probabilistic Reasoning](../chapter-13-probabilistic-reasoning/README.md)

---

## Chapter Overview

Introduce decision theory combining probability and utility. Cover preference axioms, utility functions, decision networks (influence diagrams), value of information, and risk attitudes. Apply to medical, business, and engineering decisions under uncertainty.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Explain the axioms underlying rational preference and utility
2. Construct utility functions from preferences and assess risk neutrality
3. Evaluate decision scenarios using maximum expected utility (MEU)
4. Build and solve decision networks with chance and decision nodes
5. Compute value of perfect and sample information
6. Distinguish normative vs. descriptive decision behavior
7. Model multi-attribute utility and trade-offs
