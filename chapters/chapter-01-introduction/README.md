# Chapter 01: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

> **Source:** *Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach* (4th ed.), Chapter 1
> **Part:** Part I: Artificial Intelligence
> **Estimated time:** 8–10 hours
> **Prerequisites:** Course 1: Applied ML & AI for Engineers (Chapters 01–03 minimum)

---

## Chapter Overview

Establish the intellectual foundations of AI as the study of rational agents. Survey historical milestones from symbolic AI through statistical learning to modern deep learning, examine the current state of the art across domains, and critically assess benefits, limitations, and risks including alignment and societal impact.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Define artificial intelligence and distinguish weak vs. strong AI claims
2. Explain the rational-agent framework that unifies the textbook
3. Trace key historical periods: birth, knowledge era, statistical ML, deep learning
4. Identify major application areas and benchmark achievements in the state of the art
5. Compare major AI subfields: search, logic, probability, learning, perception, robotics
6. Articulate technical and societal risks including bias, misuse, and long-term safety
7. Relate Course 1 hands-on ML experience to formal AI foundations covered here
